A free GarageBand alternative that runs in any browser

By Tongkit Leong · June 14, 2026 · 6 min read

GarageBand is excellent — but it only runs on Apple devices. If you're on Windows, Linux, or a Chromebook, Tracks is a free GarageBand-style studio that runs in any modern browser, with multi-track recording, built-in instruments, mixing, and WAV/MP3 export, and nothing to install.

The problem with most GarageBand alternatives

Search "free GarageBand for Windows" and you'll mostly find one of two things: a heavyweight DAW with a steep install and learning curve, or a watered-down online loop maker that can't export a real file. Tracks aims for the GarageBand sweet spot — approachable enough to start in minutes, capable enough to finish a song — while working on any operating system because it's just a web page.

Where a browser studio wins

  • It works everywhere. The same studio runs on a school Chromebook, a Windows laptop, a Linux box, or a Mac.
  • Zero setup. No download, no account, no license key — open a link and you're producing.
  • Always current. There's no version to update; the latest features are live the moment you load the page.
  • Private by default. Your projects never leave your device.

Where GarageBand and desktop DAWs still lead

Being honest: native apps have advantages. GarageBand ships a large library of polished loops and Apple's instruments, and desktop DAWs support third-party plugins and very large, CPU-heavy sessions. If you're scoring a film with 80 tracks of orchestral samples, a native DAW on a fast machine is the right tool. For writing, beat-making, recording demos, and finishing songs, a browser studio is faster to reach and gets the job done.

Frequently asked questions

Is Tracks free like GarageBand?

Yes — free, with no account and no paywall, on any operating system.

Can I open my GarageBand projects in Tracks?

Not directly — project formats differ. But you can export audio or MIDI from GarageBand and import it into Tracks to keep working.

Does it run on a Chromebook?

Yes. Because Tracks is a web app, it runs in Chrome on a Chromebook with nothing to install.
