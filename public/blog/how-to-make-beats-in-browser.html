How to make beats in your browser (step by step)

By Tongkit Leong · June 14, 2026 · 6 min read

To make a beat in your browser for free: open the Tracks studio, add a drum track, choose an 808 or 909 kit, tap or draw your kick, snare, and hats, then layer a bassline and melody on a synth track. No plugins and no install — it all runs on the page.

1. Set your tempo

Open the studio and set the tempo (BPM) in the transport bar. Hip-hop and trap usually sit around 80–150 BPM (trap hi-hats are often written at double-time), house lives near 120–128, and drum & bass runs 160–175. Pick a number; you can change it later, and Tracks can even ramp tempo across the song with its tempo map.

2. Lay the foundation: kick and snare

Add a drum track and open the drum machine. You get a 14-voice kit (808 or 909) mapped across pads and across labeled rows in the piano roll. Start simple: put a kick on beat 1, a snare on beats 2 and 4. That backbeat is the spine of most popular music. Tap the pads to play live, or double-click a clip and draw the hits on the grid.

3. Add movement: hats and percussion

Hi-hats create the groove. Place closed hats on the off-beats, then experiment with rolls — in trap, that means doubling or tripling hat hits into a run. Use the open hat sparingly for accents; the drum machine chokes the open hat when a closed hat fires, just like real hardware.

4. Lock in a bassline

Add a synth track, pick a bass preset, and draw a few low notes that follow your kick pattern. The simplest trick that always works: play the root note of your key on the downbeats. To stay in tune, set the project key in the transport bar so everything snaps to scale.

5. Sprinkle a melody or sample

Add another synth track for a melody or chords, or load the 16-pad sampler and drop in your own one-shots and chops. You can also slice an audio loop onto pads automatically and replay it in a new pattern.

6. Mix and export

Open the mixer, balance the levels so the kick and snare punch through, add a touch of reverb on the melodic parts, and compress the drum bus if it needs glue. When it sounds right, export to WAV for the highest quality or MP3 to share quickly.

Tips for better beats

  • Swing it. A little swing on the hats makes a stiff pattern feel human — use the swing control in the piano roll.
  • Velocity matters. Vary how hard each hit lands; flat velocities sound robotic.
  • Leave space. Silence is part of the groove. Don't fill every step.
